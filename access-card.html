Project Overview

The Biñan Access Card is a web-based government information system developed during my internship at the City Government of Biñan. The system streamlines the management and verification of resident access cards by providing an organized platform for storing, searching, and managing applicant information. It was designed to improve administrative efficiency and support the city's digital transformation initiatives.

My Responsibilities

• Designed the user interface and user experience (UI/UX) for the QR code scanner module.
• Created high-fidelity interface mockups based on the existing functional prototype.
• Improved workflow, readability, and navigation for faster beneficiary verification.
• Collaborated with the development team to ensure the proposed design aligned with system functionality.

Key Features

QR Code-Based Verification

Quickly scans and validates beneficiary QR codes, enabling fast and accurate identification during government assistance and social welfare distribution.

User-Centered Interface

Designed an intuitive and user-friendly interface that enhances the user experience for both government staff and residents, ensuring ease of use and accessibility.

Real-Time Benificiary Information

Displays essential beneficiary details immediately after scanning, allowing authorized personnel to verify records with minimal delay.

Government Service Support

Developed to enhance the Biñan Access Card System by improving the efficiency and reliability of beneficiary verification for public service operations.
