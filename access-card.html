Biñan Access Card MIS

A centralized beneficiary management platform with QR-based verification designed to support faster, more organized, and more reliable government assistance distribution.

Project Context

About the Project

The Biñan Access Card Management Information System is a web-based government platform developed during my internship at the City Government of Biñan. It centralizes beneficiary information, supports eligibility management, records assistance claims, and uses unique QR codes to streamline identity verification during government subsidy and social welfare distribution activities.

The project was developed to improve administrative efficiency, reduce duplicate or unauthorized claims, and provide authorized personnel with faster access to beneficiary records and claim history.

Problem

The Challenge

Government assistance distribution requires personnel to verify beneficiaries accurately while serving many residents within a limited period. Manual or fragmented workflows can make record searching, eligibility confirmation, claim monitoring, and duplicate-claim prevention more difficult.

  • Organize beneficiary records in one system
  • Verify identities quickly through QR codes
  • Display eligibility and claim information clearly
  • Support accurate assistance recording
  • Remain usable during actual distribution operations
Approach

The Solution

The Biñan Access Card MIS provides a centralized workflow for beneficiary registration, eligibility management, QR generation, QR verification, claim history review, and assistance recording. Its interface was designed to help authorized personnel retrieve essential information quickly and complete verification tasks with fewer unnecessary steps.

Team Contribution

My Contribution

My contribution covered both software development and interface design, with additional involvement in testing, deployment preparation, documentation, and iterative refinement with the ICTO team.
