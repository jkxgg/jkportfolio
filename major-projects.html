Biñan Access Card

Overview

The Biñan Access Card System is a government information system developed for the City Government of Biñan to modernize the management and distribution of social welfare programs. The system centralizes beneficiary information, streamlines administrative workflows, and improves the efficiency of subsidy and assistance distribution through secure QR code-based verification.

Designed for the City Social Welfare and Development (CSWD) Office, the platform replaces manual processes with a centralized digital solution that supports beneficiary record management, service administration, QR code generation, real-time transaction monitoring, and reporting. During actual distribution activities, authorized personnel use dedicated scanner stations to verify beneficiaries, while all transactions are synchronized with a central server for validation and record keeping.

Throughout my internship, I collaborated with the ICTO development team in enhancing several components of the system. My primary responsibilities focused on the QR Code Verification module, where I contributed to UI/UX improvements, validation logic, interface refinements, and database organization. I also participated in feature testing, debugging, and iterative improvements through regular team collaboration, helping improve the usability and reliability of the system before deployment.

My Contributions

• Designed the UI/UX of the QR Code Scanner module.
• Designed icons, interface elements, and visual components.
• Ensured visual consistency across the application.
• Worked closely with the development team to integrate assets into the educational platform. • Refined graphics based on project feedback and usability considerations.

K-12 Educational Game Assets

Overview

The K-12 Educational Application is an interactive learning platform commissioned by the Information and Communications Technology Office (ICTO) to support classroom instruction through engaging digital activities. Designed for Grade 5 and Grade 7 learners, the application covers Fourth Quarter curriculum topics by combining educational content with interactive gameplay, making lessons more engaging and accessible for students.

The application consists of 20 learning topics, with 10 topics each for Grade 5 and Grade 7, where every topic features three progressive difficulty levels. Each lesson integrates educational challenges, visual storytelling, and interactive mechanics to encourage active participation while reinforcing classroom learning outcomes.

As part of the development team, I was primarily responsible for producing the visual assets used throughout the application. My contributions included creating original illustrations, user interface elements, game environments, educational graphics, icons, and interactive gameplay resources. Working closely with developers and fellow interns, I ensured that every asset maintained a consistent visual style while supporting the application's educational objectives and providing an engaging experience for young learners.

My Contributions

• Created original digital illustrations and game assets..
• Designed icons, interface elements, and visual components.
• Ensured visual consistency across the application.
• Worked closely with the development team to integrate assets into the educational platform. • Refined graphics based on project feedback and usability considerations.
